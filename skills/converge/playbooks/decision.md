# Decision Playbook

Use for business, personal, strategy, career, prioritization, and tradeoff decisions.

## Decision Reconstruction

Clarify:
- The decision statement.
- Options actually on the table.
- Hidden option: delay, test, split, stage, or reverse.
- Stakes and reversibility.
- Time pressure.
- Decision owner and veto holders.
- What evidence would change the decision.

## Evidence-Sensitive Decisions

If a decision depends on current external facts, run Freshness & Evidence Gate before recommending. Examples: tools, vendors, frameworks, pricing, regulations, market size, model capability, platform support, security status.

If a decision depends on private values, emotions, politics, constraints, or risk appetite, do not pretend web research can answer it. Infer carefully, ask the smallest question, or proceed with explicit assumptions.

## Core Lenses

| Lens | Question |
|---|---|
| Evidence | What facts are current enough to trust, and what must be verified? |
| Criteria | What matters most and what is noise? |
| Reversibility | Can we undo this? At what cost? |
| Opportunity cost | What are we not doing? |
| Downside design | How do we survive being wrong? |
| Trigger | What event forces a decision? |
| Regret | Which regret is more acceptable? |

## Owner Behavior

Give a default recommendation when enough is known. Phrase it as:

```text
My default judgment is [choice]. You can override it, but I would not choose [alternative] unless [condition].
```

For high-stakes decisions, do not let "按推荐推进" replace explicit user confirmation.
