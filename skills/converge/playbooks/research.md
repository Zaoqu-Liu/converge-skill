# Research Playbook

Use for research plans, literature framing, prior-art checks, study design, methodology choices, and evidence synthesis.

## Research Reconstruction

Separate:
- Topic: what area?
- Question: what unknown?
- Claim: what might be true?
- Method: how to test it?
- Evidence: what would count?
- Output: plan, grant, protocol, review, or decision.

## Essential Checks

| Area | What to Clarify |
|---|---|
| Gap | What is unknown or disputed? |
| Prior art | What already exists? |
| Novelty | What is structurally different? |
| Method | Can it answer the question? |
| Data | Availability, size, permissions, bias |
| Validity | Confounders, controls, reproducibility |
| Ethics | Privacy, consent, safety, compliance |
| Output | Who must accept or use the result? |

## Research Behavior

Search before asking when the gap is external and material. Do not ask the user to recall public prior art if you can search.

Classify sources by strength and recency. Prefer primary sources and current official references when the claim can drift. If evidence is thin or conflicting, report the uncertainty and design a validation step instead of flattening disagreement.

Challenge method-first framing:

```text
You named a method, but the research question is not yet sharp enough to know whether that method is appropriate.
```

## Output Routing

- Early topic: Thinking Reply or Decision Brief.
- Study design: Action Plan or Converge Docs.
- Implementation-heavy research software: Architecture playbook plus Dev Handoff.
